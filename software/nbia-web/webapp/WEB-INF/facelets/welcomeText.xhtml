The Chest Image Repository (CIR) is sponsored by the National Institute for Occupational Safety and Health, which is part of the Center for Disease Control. The CIR is intended to serve as a databank of images which depict a variety of occupational respiratory diseases, particularly the pneumoconioses. The repository was developed by NIOSH to collect digitally-acquired routine chest radiographs, computerized tomography (CT) images, and associated relevant medical information, if available.

The repository images will be used by several organizations in various applications, to improve worker safety and health:

  • new reference standards for the International Labour Organization (ILO) pneumoconiosis classification
  • new digital images for the teaching syllabus, periodic examinations, and quality assurance exercises offered by the NIOSH B Reader Program
  • research into improving the identification and classification of work-related lung disease
Both domestic and international contributors are invited to submit candidate images to this repository that were obtained through worker medical screening and surveillance, clinical practice, or research activities.
